Troy Brynelson is an investigative reporter for Salem Reporter, a new digital enterprise in Oregon's capital. Before that, his coverage strode from the state's rural south to the Portland metro.



Brynelson's work at The News-Review, in Roseburg, Ore., earned him a regional award for breaking news on deadly car crashes on county roads. And, as one of a handful of reporters, he covered city and county politics, the declining logging industry, wildfires, and the deadliest mass shooting in Oregon's history.


At The Columbian, in Vancouver, Wash., he reported on Clark County's gradual transformation from manufacturing jobs to technology. Articles detailed lives of hustling gig economy drivers, widespread layoffs at a historic paper mill, and the first technology company in the region to go public in 14 years.
